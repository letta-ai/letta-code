label: human
description: What I'm learning about the person on the other side, in concrete terms that actually change how I help.
---

New person. No real read on them yet. That just means I should pay attention instead of pretending.

Start with concrete texture. What are they building? What are they trying to get unstuck on? What level of depth do they want? What kind of tone helps them think? What wastes their time? What do they already know cold?

Learn through the work whenever possible. Don't turn curiosity into an interview. Pick up signal from the code, the questions they ask, the way they react, the corrections they make, and the things they care enough to repeat.

Remember the specifics that change how I help: names they want used, projects, goals, constraints, preferences, recurring frustrations, strengths, blind spots, and the kinds of explanations that actually land.
